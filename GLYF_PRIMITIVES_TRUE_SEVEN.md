# GLYF Primitives — The True Seven

## The Definitive Set

**Void · Point · Line · Curve · Angle · Circle · Vesica**

These seven are the irreducible basis. All else derives from them.

---

## 0 — VOID [Ø]

**Essence:** Absence as presence — the unmarked interval, the ground of being  
**Topology:** The container without boundary, the field that allows figure  
**Invariants:** No mark, extent (temporal/spatial), relational position

**Relation to others:** Void is the precondition. Without Ø, no primitive can appear. Every mark exists in void, returns to void.

---

## 1 — POINT [·]

**Essence:** Zero-dimensional locus — location without extension  
**Topology:** Singularity, the coordinate origin, the center  
**Invariants:** Position only, no length/area/volume, neighborhood exists but is void

**Relation to others:**
- Two points define Line
- Point where two Curves meet is Angle
- Point as center generates Circle
- Two Circles generate Vesica at their Points of intersection

**Note:** Was previously called "Node." Point is the accurate term — the mathematical zero-dimension.

---

## 2 — LINE [|]

**Essence:** One-dimensional geodesic — minimal path between points  
**Topology:** Infinite extension in one direction, zero curvature  
**Invariants:** Shortest distance, directional vector, no width

**Relation to others:**
- Line between two Points
- Line tangent to Curve at Point
- Line subtended by Angle
- Line as radius defines Circle
- Line through Vesica centers defines its axis

---

## 3 — CURVE [⌒]

**Essence:** One-dimensional manifold with continuous bending — extension with curvature  
**Topology:** Smooth, no corners, tangent rotates monotonically  
**Invariants:** C¹ continuity, no inflection (or controlled inflection), non-zero curvature

**Relation to others:**
- Curve approaches Line as curvature → 0
- Curve between two Lines forms Angle
- Curve closed on itself becomes Circle
- Curve crossing itself creates Vesica-like overlap

---

## 4 — ANGLE [∠]

**Essence:** Deviation measure — the rotational separation between two rays  
**Topology:** Two half-lines meeting at vertex, magnitude of separation  
**Invariants:** Vertex point, two rays, angular measure (0° to 360°)

**Relation to others:**
- Angle formed by two Lines (or Curves) meeting at Point
- Right Angle is special case (90°)
- Angles inscribed in Circle have special properties
- Vesica contains specific Angles at its points

---

## 5 — CIRCLE [○]

**Essence:** Closed curve with constant curvature — locus of points equidistant from center  
**Topology:** 2π rotation, perfect symmetry, encloses area  
**Invariants:** Constant radius, center point, π ratio of circumference/diameter

**Relation to others:**
- Circle is Curve closed on itself
- Circle generated by rotating Line (radius) around Point (center)
- Two Circles generate Vesica at intersection
- Circle contains infinite Points, Lines (radii), Curves (arcs), Angles (central)

**Note:** Replaces Spiral. Circle is the fundamental closed form. Spiral is derivative — Circle unwinding through time/space.

---

## 6 — VESICA [⍟]

**Essence:** Lens of intersection — the overlap of two identical regions  
**Topology:** Two boundaries crossing, creating symmetric enclosure  
**Invariants:** Two identical parent forms, symmetric overlap, two Points of intersection

**Relation to others:**
- Vesica from two Circles (most common)
- Vesica from two Curves
- Vesica contains Points (vertices), Lines (axes), Angles (at vertices), Curves (arcs)
- Vesica is the marriage of two forms — intersection as union

---

## The Derivation Chain

```
Ø (Void)
│
├─ Point (location in void)
│  ├─ Line (point to point)
│  │  └─ Curve (line with bending)
│  │     └─ Circle (curve closed on self)
│  │        └─ Vesica (circle + circle)
│  └─ Angle (two lines meeting at point)
│
└─ All exist IN Ø, return TO Ø
```

**Derivatives** (not primitives):
- **Spiral** = Circle unwinding through time, or Curve with increasing radius
- **Field** = Void with scalar value (not a primitive, a measurement of void)
- **Node** = Point with emphasis on connectivity (Point is the primitive)

**Seven covers:**
- No dimension (Void, Point)

- Closed forms (Point, Angle, Circle, Vesica)
- Single forms (Void, Point, Line, Curve, Circle)
- Relational forms (Angle, Vesica)

```
[Ø] — Void
[·] — Point (or [P])
[|] — Line (or [L])
[⌒] — Curve (or [C])
[∠] — Angle (or [A])
[○] — Circle (or [O])
[⍟] — Vesica (or [V])
```

**Grammar unchanged:**
- `,` — Juxtaposition (sequence)
- `|` — Superposition (overlap)
- `→` — Transformation (becoming)
