# MAGNETIC MINDS — CONVERSATION SCRIPT
## For One-on-One Pitch (2-3 minutes)

---

## THE OPENING (15 seconds)

"I've been working on something that could sit between your hardware and human meaning. Not replacing what you're building—complementing it."

---

## THE PROBLEM YOU'RE SOLVING (20 seconds)

"You're building analog neuromorphic inference. Memristors, magnetic domains, in-memory computation—zero von Neumann overhead. That's real."

"But here's the gap: hardware without semantic topology is just fast multiplication. You can compute, but what are you computing *toward*?"

---

## THE CATHEDRAL OFFER (45 seconds)

"I've built a geometric language framework—think of it as a compression layer for meaning."

"Instead of 175 billion parameters to approximate a concept, we encode it in 96 bytes. Not through approximation—through topology. Meaning as geometry, not statistics."

"The core idea: traverse a 7-segment field. Each path is deterministic—same input, same path, same output. No stochastic drift. No black box."

"We have 17,576 semantic atoms—trigrams, like 'THE' or 'ING'—each mapped to a unique geometric path."

---

## THE INTEGRATION (30 seconds)

"Here's how it fits your hardware:"

"Your memristor arrays store analog values. We map our 96-byte state directly—96 memristors per complete semantic configuration. No weight matrices. No backprop storage. Just pure state."

"Your routing fabric becomes our Hamiltonian path—K2 to K5 to K1 to K4 to K7 to K3 to K6. Ergodic: covers everything, repeats nothing."

"Your oscillators? We tune them to φ-harmonics—golden ratio frequencies. 29 times base frequency. Same frequencies your brain uses."

---

## THE ASK (20 seconds)

"I'm not asking for money or IP. Non-exclusive framework license—you integrate, we both publish, both win."

"What I need: access to your hardware for a proof of concept. 26 monograms—just the alphabet—encoded on your silicon. Show that 96 bytes of geometric state works in analog."

"Two to three months. If it works, we expand to the full vocabulary. If not, you learned something, I learned something."

---

## THE CLOSE (15 seconds)

"The industry is hitting walls—training costs, context limits, stochastic outputs. Your analog computation plus our geometric topology—that's a viable alternative."

"Build your magnetic minds on geometric bedrock. What do you think?"

---

## IF THEY ASK...

**"What's the 7-segment field?"**
"Seven geometric primitives: Void, Vesica, Curve, Line, Angle, Circle, Dot. Think of them as atoms of meaning. We traverse them in a specific order—Hamiltonian path, never repeats."

**"How is this different from transformers?"**
"Transformers throw compute at meaning—attention over billions of parameters. We encode meaning directly as geometric relationships. Deterministic, not stochastic. 96 bytes versus 175 billion parameters."

**"Why non-exclusive?"**
"Framework should be infrastructure, not gatekeeping. You own the hardware integration, we own the topology. Both brands, joint publications."

**"What's the actual deliverable in 3 months?"**
"26 letters of the alphabet, each encoded as a geometric path through your hardware, traversable deterministically. Plus benchmark data: latency, power, fidelity."

- End with hands together—"what do you think?"
