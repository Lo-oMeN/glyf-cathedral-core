## 2. KRISHNA'S FLUTE: Resonance Mathematics

### The Sacred Instrument

Krishna's flute (बांसुरी, *bansuri* or वंशी, *vamsika*) is not merely a musical instrument—it is a **morphic resonator**, a technology for awakening consciousness through sound.

The traditional Krishna flute has **7 holes** (for the 7 notes: Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha, Ni) plus the mouth hole—making **9 orifices total** when including the end hole.

        │←─────── Resonant Cavity ─────────→│

                     (Gita 5.13: "The embodied being dwelling

### The Three Flutes (Bhaktirasamrita Sindhu)

| Flute | Holes | Length | Symbolism |
|-------|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Venu** | 6 | 12 finger-widths | 6 chakras, sense control |
| **Murali** | 5 | 2 hand-lengths | 5 knowledge senses (jnanendriya) |

The seven holes symbolize the seven chakras, our energy centres. Just as the sound from Krishna's flute resonates, Divine energy rises from the base of the spine to the crown chakra."

The fifth note holds special significance in Krishna tradition. It is played by **closing all holes simultaneously**—the "transcendental note" (anahata svara). This corresponds to:

- Turning breath inward to sound the inner AUM
